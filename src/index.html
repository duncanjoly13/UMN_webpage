I joined the Networking Research Group during my second semester as an undergraduate student under the mentorship of Jason Carpenter. I am broadly interested in mobile network security and availability, Internet access equality, and privacy in computer networks. In addition to my position as a researcher, I am the Communications Subsystem Leader at the University's Small Satellite Research Laboratory where I manage and develop the systems related to information exchange between our satellites and Earth.

I am pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science at the University, and will graduate in Fall 2025.

Research

At the beginning of my second year as an undergraduate, I published Examining Interplay of Compression and Encryption and Applicability to 5G Teleoperations with co-authors Jason Carpenter and Dr. Zhi-Li Zhang. I am currently assisting research efforts with Dr. Eman Ramadan, Rostand A. K. Fezeu, Faaiq Bilal, and others.
